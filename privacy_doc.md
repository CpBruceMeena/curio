### 4. Advertising

**Types of Ads**

Curio may display the following ad formats:
- **Native Ads:** Appearing between content cards in the Infinite Feed
- **Interstitial Ads:** Appearing between puzzles or after 5-10 feed items
- **Banner Ads:** At the bottom of Discover and Bookmarks screens
- **Rewarded Ads:** Offering puzzle hints or premium content in exchange for viewing

**Ad Providers**

Curio uses Google AdMob as its primary advertising partner. AdMob may collect:
- Device identifiers (Advertising ID)
- IP address (anonymized)
- Coarse location (inferred from IP)
- App usage data for ad personalization

**User Controls**

You can:
- Reset your Advertising ID in Android Settings
- Opt out of ad personalization in Android Settings → Google → Ads
- Disable ad tracking entirely (though ads will still appear, they will be less relevant)

**Children's Privacy**

Curio does not knowingly serve personalized ads to users under 13. If you are under 13, please do not consent to ad personalization when prompted.

### 5. Offline Content Caching

**How Offline Access Works**

To provide offline access and reduce server load, Curio downloads content to your device in the background when you have an internet connection. This includes:
- Facts
- Short stories and novels
- Poems and Shayari
- Puzzles
- Audio files (text-to-speech)

**Storage Location**

All cached content is stored in **app-specific internal storage** (`Context.getFilesDir()`). This means:
- No special storage permissions are required
- Content is automatically deleted when you uninstall the App
- Curio cannot access cached content from other apps
- Other apps cannot access Curio's cached content

**Your Control**

You can:
- Clear all cached content anytime: Settings → Storage → Clear Cache
- Choose to cache only on Wi-Fi: Settings → Data Usage → Cache on Wi-Fi Only
- Disable background caching entirely (requires re-enabling consent)

**Consent Requirement**

Before any background caching begins, you will see a prominent disclosure dialog asking for your consent. You may decline, in which case:
- Curio will only function when online
- Certain features may be unavailable

### 6. User-Generated Content (Journal)

**Privacy Guarantee**

Your journal entries:
- Are stored **only on your device**
- Are never transmitted to Curio's servers
- Are never shared with third parties
- Are cleared when you uninstall the App

**Data Retention**

Journal data remains on your device indefinitely until you:
- Delete individual entries
- Clear app data via Android Settings
- Uninstall the App

### 7. Device Identifier (UUID)

**How Curio Identifies You**

On first launch, Curio generates a random UUID (Universally Unique Identifier) stored in the App's local storage. This identifier:
- Is completely anonymous (no personal information attached)
- Persists across app restarts
- Is deleted when you clear app data or uninstall

**What the UUID Is Used For**

- Saving your bookmarks and preferences
- Storing your profile information (name, age, interests — you provide these voluntarily)
- Attribution for feedback submissions
- Personalizing content recommendations

**What the UUID Is NOT Used For**

- Tracking you across other apps or websites
- Identifying you personally without additional voluntary data
- Any form of cross-device tracking
